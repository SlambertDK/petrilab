PetriLab

A living laboratory for open-ended evolution — a digital petri dish where complexity is grown, measured, and kept honest. Built to explore one of the hardest open questions in AI: how do you make a system that never stops inventing?

The dream

What if a system could keep surprising us — forever?

Nature never converged. Four billion years in, it is still inventing new forms. Almost every system we build does the opposite: it optimises, plateaus, and stops. PetriLab is a sandbox to chase the exception — open-ended evolution: novelty that accumulates instead of settling.

  • Not "find the best solution." That's optimisation — it ends.
  • Instead "never stop finding genuinely new ones." That's open-endedness — the frontier.
Why it matters

The same question sits under the AI we actually build

Creativity that lasts

Models that plateau are a business problem. Understanding what keeps a system generative is directly useful for how we deploy AI.

Emergence, made visible

Complex behaviour from simple rules — the core mystery of modern AI. PetriLab lets you watch it happen and measure it.

A safe place to be wrong

A sandbox where a failed hypothesis costs nothing and teaches everything. Innovation practice, not innovation theatre.

What it is

A dish full of tiny organisms — alive on screen

Hundreds of cells with heritable genomes. They divide, mutate, wire up into tissue, signal each other, and compete for energy through changing seasons. No cell is designed. Everything you see emerged from local rules.

Open genome

Variable-length genetic code that can grow, duplicate and shed genes. The space of possible organisms is unbounded.

Lineages

Every cell remembers its ancestry. We can watch family lines rise, compete, and die — the raw material of evolution.

The gardener

An autonomous experimenter tends the dish

PetriLab runs itself. A "gardener" agent watches the ecosystem and runs its own experiments — a visible, self-improving loop:

1 → 2

Hypothesis → Test

Forms an idea, then tunes exactly one environmental ratio. Never the rules, never the DNA.

3

Measure

Watches what the change did to the ecosystem's health over time.

4

Keeps what helped, reverts what didn't — and biases future experiments toward knobs that work.

The long-term vision: the gardener searches the web to validate its own ideas and design richer experiments.

What we prove

We refuse to fool ourselves

The easiest trap in this field is measuring success by your own definition. PetriLab ships with a falsification contract: three independent tests, decoupled from the novelty metric, that must all pass before we claim open-ended evolution.

Lineage survival

Do multiple family lines persist — or does one take over? (Monoculture = failure.)

Novelty alive

Is the system still minting genuinely new macro-states?

Not periodic

Is that novelty real accumulation — not a seasonal cycle in disguise?

Evidence · a real result

The dish collapsed — and told us so

Left running, PetriLab evolved toward monoculture: after 800,000 generations, one lineage had out-competed all others. Novelty, change and ecology all fell to zero. The falsification contract flipped to FAILURE — exactly as it should.

1

lineage left, down from 24. Diversity dead.

0.00

novelty, change, ecology. Nothing new happening.

6+

lineages held after the fix — verified across 40,000 generations.

The fix — immigration: inject fresh lineages when diversity collapses. Raw material only; selection untouched. Verdict back to success.

The lesson

Build the measuring instrument before the experiment — so the system can prove itself wrong, instead of quietly confirming what you hoped for.

That discipline is the transferable product lesson: honest metrics that can fail are worth more than dashboards that always look green.
